So there's a company called QuTwo that just got valued at $380 million before it's even really off the ground. That's what an "angel round" means — early believers putting in money when the product is still being built. The interesting part isn't the dollar figure though, it's what they're actually building: they combine quantum computing with AI, but their founder is quick to say "we're an AI company, quantum is just how we run the calculations." Think of it like this — most AI today runs on regular computer chips, like cooking on a gas stove. Quantum computing is more like a commercial kitchen with equipment most people have never seen. QuTwo wants to use that fancier kitchen to serve AI to big businesses faster and more powerfully.

The reason this matters for regular people is that enterprise AI — the kind big companies use to run their operations, analyze data, and automate decisions — is about to get a serious upgrade. When the underlying computing gets faster and cheaper, the tools eventually trickle down. The software your bank uses, the logistics behind your Amazon delivery, the insurance quote you get online — all of that runs on enterprise AI. Better compute means those systems get smarter, and eventually the tools small businesses can afford get better too.

So how does this actually help you right now?

If you run a small business, keep an eye on enterprise AI tools that are starting to offer "lite" versions of what big companies use. Platforms like Microsoft Copilot for business or Google's Workspace AI are already doing this. Spending an hour learning one of these could save you five hours a week on emails, reports, and scheduling — easily worth $200-500 a month in your own time back.

If you're thinking about where to grow your skills, enterprise AI is where the real hiring is happening. You don't need to be an engineer. Companies desperately need people who understand both the business side and the AI side — project managers, trainers, and "AI translators" who help teams actually use these tools. A $200 online course today could land you a $15,000 salary bump within a year.

If you invest, even small amounts through platforms like Robinhood or Fidelity, watch the companies building infrastructure for AI — not just the flashy chatbot apps. The picks-and-shovels businesses (chips, cloud computing, data centers) tend to be more stable long-term bets as AI demand grows.

The companies writing the big checks today are telling you exactly where the money will flow tomorrow — pay attention.
