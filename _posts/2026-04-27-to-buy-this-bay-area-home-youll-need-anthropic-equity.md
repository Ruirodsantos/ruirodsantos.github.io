Here's something you don't see every day in real estate listings: a seller who doesn't want your cash, your mortgage approval letter, or your crypto. They want equity in Anthropic, the AI company behind the Claude chatbot. Basically, if you happen to be an early Anthropic employee sitting on a pile of unvested or restricted company shares, this seller in Mill Valley, California would rather take those than dollars. Think of it like a farmer trading land for gold nuggets instead of paper money — except the gold nuggets are shares in a company that isn't publicly traded yet and could be worth a fortune, or could take years to cash out.

Why would someone do this? Because Anthropic is one of the hottest AI companies around right now, valued at tens of billions of dollars, but its shares aren't available on any stock exchange. The only people who have them are employees and early investors. If you're a seller who believes Anthropic is going to keep growing, taking equity instead of cash is basically a bet that those shares will eventually be worth more than whatever the house sells for today. It's the same logic as taking stock options instead of salary at a startup — risky, but potentially very rewarding if the company takes off. The seller is essentially saying, "I'd rather own a piece of the AI future than have money sitting in a bank account."

So what does this mean for you practically? A few ideas worth thinking about:

If you work at any growing tech or AI startup and you're weighing salary versus equity in your compensation package, this story is a good reminder that equity can have real-world purchasing power, sometimes even before a company goes public. Negotiate for it.

If you're a small business owner or freelancer who works with AI companies or their employees, consider whether you could offer services in exchange for equity or revenue share rather than flat fees. Some startups will do this, especially early on.

If you have any exposure to pre-IPO companies through platforms like Hiive, EquityZen, or Forge Global, you can actually buy shares in private companies like Anthropic as an accredited investor. It's not for everyone, but it's a real thing regular people with some savings can explore, not just Silicon Valley insiders.

The real lesson here is simple: in a world where AI companies are minting paper millionaires before they ever go public, equity is becoming its own currency — and the smart move is figuring out how to get some of it working for you.
