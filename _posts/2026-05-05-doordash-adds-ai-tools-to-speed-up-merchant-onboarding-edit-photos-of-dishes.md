So DoorDash just made it a lot easier for restaurants to get up and running on their platform. Basically, they've added a few AI helpers behind the scenes: one that speeds up the paperwork and setup process when a new restaurant joins, one that touches up food photos automatically to make dishes look more appealing, and one that can build a basic website for a restaurant using information it already has. Think of it like having a patient assistant who fills out your forms, a food stylist who polishes your photos, and a web designer who works while you sleep — all rolled into one and available the moment you sign up.

The photo editing piece is actually the most interesting part here. Bad food photos are genuinely one of the biggest reasons people scroll past a menu item. If you've ever tried to photograph your home cooking and ended up with something that looks like a crime scene, you know what restaurants deal with every day. AI can adjust lighting, color, and contrast in seconds to make a plate of pasta look like it belongs in a magazine. It won't fix bad food, but it can absolutely fix a bad photo — and for small restaurant owners without a marketing budget, that's a real and practical upgrade.

So how could this actually help you, either as a customer or a small business owner?

If you own a restaurant or food business, get on DoorDash now while these tools are free or bundled in. Use the AI photo editing on your top 10 selling items and see if your order volume changes. Many small restaurants see a direct lift in sales just from better images — it's one of the cheapest wins available.

If you run any kind of local business with a bad or nonexistent website, watch what DoorDash is doing closely. Similar AI website-building tools exist right now through platforms like Wix, Squarespace, and Shopify — they can generate a starter site from just a few sentences about your business. You could have something live this weekend for under $20 a month.

If you're a freelancer or side hustler, there's a small opportunity to become the person who helps local restaurants actually use these tools. Many owners are too busy cooking to learn new software. Offering a simple "DoorDash setup and photo optimization" service for a flat fee could be a genuine earner in your neighborhood.

Better photos make people hungry, and hungry people spend money — that's really all there is to it.
