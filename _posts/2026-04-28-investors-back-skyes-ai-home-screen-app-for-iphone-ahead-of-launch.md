So imagine your phone's home screen knew you well enough to surface the right app, reminder, or piece of information before you even thought to look for it. That's essentially what Skye is building — an AI layer that sits on top of your iPhone experience and tries to anticipate what you need next. Think of it like having a really attentive personal assistant who's memorized your habits, except it lives in your pocket and doesn't need a coffee break. The fact that investors put money in before anyone could even download the app tells you how eager people are to make phones feel smarter in a genuinely useful way, not just a "hey here's a fun chatbot" way.

The interesting thing here isn't really about Skye specifically — it's about the broader shift happening with how we interact with our phones. Right now, your home screen is basically a grid of icons you tap through out of muscle memory. AI home screen apps are trying to change that into something more like a conversation, where your phone responds to your context and routines rather than waiting for you to remember which app to open. It's the difference between a filing cabinet and a really good secretary.

So how does this actually help you, financially speaking? A few ways worth thinking about.

First, if you're a freelancer or run a small business, keep an eye on apps like this when they launch. Tools that reduce the time you spend context-switching between apps — jumping from calendar to email to notes to invoices — are time savers, and time is money. Even getting back 20 minutes a day adds up to real hours each week.

Second, if you're someone who invests in individual stocks or follows tech trends, the investor interest in Skye signals that AI on mobile is heating up. Apple, Google, and Samsung are all going to be competing hard in this space. Following where early funding is going can help you make smarter decisions about which tech companies might grow.

Third, small business owners could use this category of app to build better client-facing experiences. As AI home screens become more common, customers will expect their apps and services to feel more proactive. Getting ahead of that expectation now — even just by setting up better automated follow-ups or smarter scheduling — puts you in a stronger position.

Your phone is about to get a lot better at reading your mind, so you might as well figure out how to profit from that before everyone else does.
