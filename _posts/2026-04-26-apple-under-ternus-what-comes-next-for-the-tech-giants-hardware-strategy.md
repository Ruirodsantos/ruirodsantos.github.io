So Apple might be getting a new CEO who spent his career obsessing over how things feel in your hand rather than how they perform on a spreadsheet. John Ternus, who's been leading Apple's hardware engineering, is the kind of person who loses sleep over the weight of a hinge or the click of a button. If he takes the top job, it's a signal that Apple wants to get back to what made people fall in love with the brand in the first place — objects that feel genuinely special to use. Think less "services subscription" energy and more "unboxing something that makes you gasp."

What does this mean practically? It suggests Apple may start pouring more attention into making its physical devices feel worth the price again. There's been a quiet frustration among longtime Apple fans that recent years felt a bit cautious — iterative updates, modest leaps. A hardware-focused leader tends to push for bigger swings on the actual product. We might see more ambitious designs, stronger bets on spatial computing like the Vision Pro, or Apple finally cracking something genuinely new in the home device space. It's like when a restaurant that got distracted with its catering business brings back the original chef — suddenly the food gets interesting again.

Now here's how you can actually use this information to your advantage. First, if you're a small business that does any kind of tech reselling or refurbishing — phones, tablets, laptops — keep a close eye on Apple's next product cycle. Leadership transitions often come with a splashy product push, and older models tend to drop in price right before or after a big launch. That's your buying window. Second, if you're a freelancer or creator who's been holding off on upgrading your setup, now might be a smart time to wait rather than buy. A hardware-focused CEO is more likely to greenlight something worth upgrading to, so patience could mean you skip a generation and land on something genuinely better. Third, if you sell anything on Etsy, eBay, or your own site that relates to Apple products — cases, accessories, tutorials, repair guides — a renewed public excitement around Apple hardware is good for your traffic. People googling new Apple devices also click on accessory listings and how-to content. Ride that wave early by creating content or listings before the hype peaks.

The bottom line: leadership changes at big companies are usually snooze-worthy corporate news, but when the new boss is a product person, that's worth watching with your wallet open.
