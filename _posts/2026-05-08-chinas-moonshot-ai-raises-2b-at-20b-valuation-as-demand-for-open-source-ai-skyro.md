You know how some software is free to download and tinker with, while other software locks you behind a paywall and keeps all the secret sauce hidden? That same split exists in AI, and right now the "free and open" side is winning big. Moonshot AI, a Chinese company, just raised two billion dollars because businesses and developers are rushing toward AI tools they can actually look under the hood of, customize, and build on top of. Open source AI means the underlying model is available for anyone to use, modify, and deploy — kind of like getting the actual recipe instead of just the finished meal.

What's driving this money flood is pretty straightforward: companies don't want to be forever dependent on one AI provider who can change prices or terms whenever they feel like it. When you can run your own version of an AI model, you're in control. Moonshot's growth — they're pulling in over $200 million a year in subscription and API fees — tells you that even with free open source options available, plenty of people will pay for a well-packaged, reliable version of that technology. Think of it like Linux: technically free, but Red Hat built a billion-dollar business helping companies actually use it.

So what does this mean for your wallet? First, if you run any kind of small business, now is a genuinely good time to explore open source AI tools like Llama or Mistral that you can use without paying per-query fees. A small retail shop could set up a customer FAQ chatbot for close to zero ongoing cost instead of paying monthly SaaS fees. Second, if you have even basic technical curiosity, learning to work with AI APIs — even just knowing how to connect one to a spreadsheet or simple website — is a skill that small businesses are actively paying freelancers for right now, often $50 to $150 an hour. Third, watch for the wave of affordable products being built on top of these open models. Specialized tools for lawyers, tradespeople, tutors, and bookkeepers are popping up at much lower price points than the big-name AI products, because their builders have lower underlying costs. Switching from a $100/month AI writing tool to a $12/month open-source-backed alternative doing the same job is real money back in your pocket.

The companies placing big bets on open AI aren't doing it out of generosity — they see the same opportunity you now have access to.
